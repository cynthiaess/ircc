WURD reporting
Q2 2023-2024

Helping users and navigating change

IRCC is experiencing a transformative shift with the introduction of the Client eXperience Platform (CXP). The Web UX Research and Design (WURD) team is on the front line working to:

  • use evidence to keep improving Tier 1 web content
  • ensure a smooth transition towards the CXP
  • have a positive impact on the overall client journey

Key highlights

Alignment with DPM3: We made significant progress in aligning our priorities with DPM3’s work on the CXP. We’re collaborating closely with DPM3 to ensure a smooth transition.

Addressing pain points: Insights from the GC Task Success Survey and the Client Experience Survey suggest that finding general information on programs, signing in to an account, and applying for a visitor visa are major pain points. Our projects focus on these (and more).

Preparing for the future: The groundwork we laid this quarter will create a solid foundation for the upcoming transition, while preserving a strong and useful non-authenticated presence.

Explore immigration programs tool

  • 1 Research
  • 2 Design
  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Description

New tool that:

  • addresses the challenges of the Come to Canada tool
  • offers a broad, high-level overview of different programs to help clients self-serve

What we did

Over the summer, we:

  • tested our prototype with real clients
  • improved the tool based on feedback and performance

We are now in final translations and preparations for launch.

Next steps

We intend to launch the tool on Canada.ca in October. We’ll have measures in place to monitor its performance and improve it further (if needed). Our data sources will be:

  • a standardized, validated user experience survey
  • an analytics dashboard to monitor web traffic and tool usage
  • the Page feedback tool (implemented on all the tool’s screens)
  • a broader environmental scan, looking at social media and other non-Canada.ca channels for client comments and feedback about the Explore immigration programs tool

What we learned

  • Through usability testing, we found that clients:
    • were sensitive to any information that seemed like eligibility criteria
    • would attempt to self-determine their eligibility based on incomplete information
  • We improved the Explore immigration programs tool by:
    • reducing the overall amount of content
    • making it explicitly clear that clients must continue to the “How to Apply” pages to determine their eligibility

Sign in

  • 1 Research
  • 2 Design
  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Description

Redesign of the IRCC sign-in experience to:

  • understand the current account ecosystem
  • make it easier for clients to sign in to their existing accounts
  • prepare for the incoming Client eXperience Platform (CXP)

What we did

To understand what clients experience, we:

  • mapped all existing accounts and the tasks in each
  • analyzed the current site experience
  • reviewed data from a mix of sources:
    • feedback tools (GC Task Success Survey, Page feedback tool, Qualtrics surveys)
    • web analytics
    • social media and online forums
    • Client Support Centre call data

Using this information, we:

  • developed a new wizard to help clients
  • easily access their account
  • find the account they need (if they don’t remember where they were)
  • drafted new pages that list all IRCC accounts (based on client type: applicant, representative or partner)
  • started building a prototype of this wizard

Next steps

  • Complete the wizard prototype and test it with real clients (working with the CEB usability team).
    • Updates will be made based on the testing results.
  • Identify the pages we will add a general “Sign in” button to, which will direct clients to the sign-in wizard.
  • Launch the sign-in wizard, button and account pages.
  • Monitor for any issues and feedback, and update as needed.

What we learned

We have a more complete understanding of our clients’:

  • experiences with signing in to our multiple accounts
  • difficulties with figuring this out on their own (since it was a challenge for us to figure it out)

We also recognize that introducing the CXP will temporarily increase confusion for clients, even though the end goal is to streamline and simplify sign in.

Visitor visa

  • 1 Research
  • 2 Design
  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Description

Short-term project to:

  • prepare the visitor visa How to Apply pages for first onboarding into the CXP
  • streamline instructions and guidance so clients have a better experience with our site

What we did

To prepare for CXP onboarding, we:

  • researched to better understand when clients should be sent to which account and what documents they need to apply (based on their situations)
  • prototyped a more streamlined version of the How to apply page, using the newer subway navigation pattern
  • collaborated with DMP3 to make sure the prototype enables a smooth onboarding of the right clients to the CXP

Next steps

  • Get the prototype approved by all relevant stakeholders.
  • Get the prototype live in advance of the CXP release 1 date (November 21).
  • Monitor feedback and analytics for issues and opportunities for improvement.

What we learned

  • We need to work closely with DPM3. This will be crucial in future releases as more How to Apply pages (for other lines of businesses) will be impacted.
  • The narrow criteria for CXP Release 1 (new clients, applying for themselves only, over 18, and not traveling as a group) force everyone to go through many questions, making it more complicated to get the right information on how to apply.
