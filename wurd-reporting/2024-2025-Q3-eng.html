Sign in

  • 1 Research
  • 2 Design

  • 5 Monitoring

Impact on the user journey

  1. Seek
  2. Apply (impacted)
  3. Wait (impacted)
  4. Move forward

Statistics

  • Over 8.7 million visits since full release of version 3 on September 3, 2024
  • 81% of users who arrived at the account wizard tool started through the tool, with 94% completing it through to an account result
  • Roughly 31% of users try to sign in on mobile
  • 82.5% of users move to the IRCC secure account, 8.9% go to the IRCC Portal, and 5% move to program content
  • About 89.5% want to sign in, and 10.5% want to create an account.

Source: Adobe Analytics

Description

  • Redesign of the IRCC sign-in experience to:
    • understand the current account ecosystem
    • make it easier for clients to sign in to their existing accounts
    • prepare for the incoming Client eXperience Platform (CXP)

What we did

  • Provided advice to the Web Operations – Enterprise team about how to implement the sign-in button across more of the site and where to point it to.
  • Continued to monitor user activity and feedback.

Next steps

  • Plan and develop version 4 of the wizard, which will integrate initial account triaging for new applicants, and linking paper applications.

What we learned

  • The majority of our clients continue to use the IRCC secure account to apply, even if it’s not the account we tell them to use in program content.
  • Clientscontinue to want to start with account creation, and look for the language to match.
  • Clients just want to sign in and don’t understand why we can’t simplify to that. They don’t understand the multiple account situation and how it complicates their experience.

My application

  • 1 Research
  • 2 Design
  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Statistics

  • Over 845,000 visits to the new design since full launch on December 9, 2024.
  • A decrease of 16% in users reporting they didn’t find what they were looking for on the page.
  • A 10% decrease in the number of users leaving comments on the page.

Source: Adobe Analytics

Description

  • Project to investigate client pain points and other issues related to general application content and how to improve the My application topic page.

What we did

  • Completed a first-click test for the mobile design, asking real users to identify what they would select on the page to complete pre-determined tasks.
  • Analyzed results from both the desktop and mobile tests to fine-tune the new design.
  • Launched the new design on November 18, 2024, using Adobe Target to slowly roll it out to all users. Moved to 100% visibility on the site on December 9, 2024.

Next steps

  • Continue to monitor new page design.
  • Continue research into user needs and options for a general application site section.
  • Continue analysis of general application information in the Help Centre and develop a plan to merge that content into the main site.

What we learned

  • Accounts (and technical sign-in issues), application status, contacting IRCC and processing times continue to be issues that web can’t fully resolve.

Settlement topic

  • 1 Research
  • 2 Design
  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Statistics

  • The PFT no clicks per 1000 visits on the Settlement topic page reduced from 4.08 to 1.72 about 2 weeks after its launch.

Source: Adobe Analytics

Description

  • After creating a report for the advisors on the Settlement IA, we were asked to provide support in implementing recommendations.

What we did

  • Evaluated the new topic page template that flattens the IA against the current one. To do this, we:
    • created top tasks based on user behavior and advisor priorities
    • created prototypes of the current IA and the new IA in Figma
    • performed tests on each prototype for the tasks
    • produced a report that compared their results. The results of the test concluded that the proposed IA would likely perform better than the current one.
  • Created an analytics report about 2 weeks after launch to measure the impact of the changes.

What we learned

  • How to create a prototype to perform unmoderated tests with UXTweak and Figma.
  • How to interpret UXTweak prototype test results.
  • Creating reports to summarize prototype test results and present to advisors.

PR Card review

  • 1 Research
  • 2 Design
  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Description

  • WURD was approached to do a heuristic (expert) review of revised PR card content

What we did

  • Revised the content (plain language review)
  • We created Figma prototypes
    • An option using "cards"
    • A field flow option
  • We also created an HTML prototype of the field flow option

Next steps

  • The web advisors on the file are validating the approach with the subject matter experts

What we learned

  • Some are hesitant to use the field flow approach to display content. However, given how our programs are designed, it can sometimes be a helpful way to guide users to the right application process. For the PR card, the goal was to let users know they might not need to apply for one for the first time, preventing them from accidentally starting a process that doesn’t apply to them.
  • Web advisors have mentioned that clients like to see HTML prototypes as it tends to speed up the review process
    • The ability for WURD advisors to be able to do a variety of tasks (from content creation all the way to HTML prototyping) is an asset for complex projects.

  • 2 Design
  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Statistics

  • In the first-click test of the topic page, all tasks (except one) surpassed our goal of a task success rate above 50%.

Source: Adobe Analytics

Description

  • Redesign of citizenship topic page and citizenship test content, including Help Centre questions and tutorial content.

What we did

  • Redesigned citizenship topic page, conducted a first-click test and shared results with clients in a report
  • Designed mock-ups of a citizenship test subway on Figma after having:
    • mapped out current information architecture (IA)
    • conducted research using web analytics, Google Search data, Page feedback tool, and Task success survey

Next steps

  • Share citizenship test mock-ups with WURD team for an expert review
  • Get feedback from program clients on mock-ups
  • Finalize content with web advisor
  • Complete research report with design recommendations

What we learned

  • Involve program clients early
  • Use Figma for collaboration and iterating designs
  • Users had problems with the following content:
    • Test results and next steps
    • Practice tests or study guide
    • Technical difficulties
    • Test invitation and test link

WURD impact assessment

Description

  • After checking the progress of WURD’s recommendations, we decided to improve how we work with advisors with the goal of making implementing our recommendations easier for them.

What we did

  • Mapped out all recommendations from previous research reports made by WURD
  • Brainstormed questions to ask advisors on how to improve our ways of working and interacting with them

Next steps

  • Send out a survey to advisors, and conduct interviews with advisors we have worked with, as well as those we are yet to work with.
  • Create a document outlining what we have learned, and recommendations for how we should improve our methods of working with advisors and delivering our services

What we learned

  • There are a lot of factors that affect how quickly advisors can fully implement recommendations. Understanding these factors can make our work with them smoother and more pleasant
  • Getting anonymous feedback from advisors might lead to useful feedback that they might not be comfortable sharing directly with us
  • There is still room for improvement in the way we work with advisors and deliver our services

What we did

CxP for TRV

  • On November 4, 2024, we ended the target activity for TRV and integrated the CxP option to the website for users.
  • Continue to provide weekly stats updates as well as monitor comments from the page feedback tool

CxP for passport renewals

  • Liaised with DPM to solidify content for online renewals
  • On December 11, 2024, we launched the online application option – targeted to 1% of users who access the passport renewals page. On January 8, 2025, we increased this activity to 2%.

  • Created an Adobe Analytics dashboard for the DPM team to access as needed (while staying a trusted resource for analysis of data).

Other:

Next steps

  • Continue to provide weekly stats updates
  • Continue to monitor the online environment around online passport renewals
  • Be present and ready when the target activity needs to increase in percentage or be removed
  • Create webpage to support project 3

What we learned

  • The experience with TRV has stabilized, with the click through rate to CxP sitting at 11%.
  • Clients are keen to apply for a passport online, but have expressed frustration that they can’t find information or the offer on our website to apply online (due to not receiving the targeted activity with online renewals).
