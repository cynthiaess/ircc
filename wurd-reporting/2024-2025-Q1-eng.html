Year 2 of WURD: tackling complex issues

As we started our second year of existence, WURD tackled some of the complex issues that impact user experience, like the sign in ecosystem, important programs like work permits and Express Entry, information related to related to applications, and campaign pages.

We also supported Release 1 of the Client Experience Platform, while fostering a culture of UX excellence in Web communications by developing a playbook and a web performance dashboard.

Key highlights

  • Launched a new Sign-in wizard to help people find the right account
  • Completed phase 2 of the Explore immigration programs tool
  • Delivered multiple research projects with tens of actionable, evidence-based recommendations

Impact on the user journey

  1. Seek
  2. Apply (impacted)
  3. Wait (impacted)

Statistics
  • Over 80% of users who arrived at the account wizard tool started through the tool, with over 90% completing it through to an account result
  • Roughly 28% of users try to sign in on mobile
  • Roughly 75% of users move to the IRCC secure account
  • Over 40% of users are signing in for visitor visa applications

Source: Adobe Analytics

Description

Redesign of the IRCC sign-in experience to:

  • understand the current account ecosystem
  • make it easier for clients to sign in to their existing accounts
  • prepare for the incoming Client eXperience Platform (CXP)

What we did

  • Rolled out an account wizard to help IRCC clients get back to their account sign in page.
    • Monitored feedback and user activity, providing weekly updates for 6 weeks after launch.
  • Developed and rolled out a version 2 of the wizard, based on user feedback, to simplify and streamline the experience for clients
    • Version 2 overtook the initial wizard within a week of launching.
    • Monitored feedback and user activity.
  • Liaised with Principal Publisher and CDS to update the canada.ca megamenu and the GC accounts page, to point the IRCC sign-in links to the account wizard.
  • Began developing a version 3 of the wizard, based on updated user feedback.

Next steps

  • Finalize, seek approval and launch version 3 of the account wizard
  • Continue monitoring analytics and feedback data for the IRCC account wizard.

What we learned

  • The majority of our clients use the IRCC secure account to apply, even if it’s not the account we tell them to use in program content.
  • Clients refer to the secure account as the GCKey account and look for that language.
  • There are 4 main applications people sign in to an account for.
  • Iterating, rather than trying to perfect a product before launch, is a faster way to understand what our clients need and how they use our tools.

Description

  • Project to:
    • support the development of the new client platform for IRCC
    • coordinate the integration of this new platform into the existing IRCC website

What we did

  • Provided support and guidance to accommodate last-minute changes for eta-X countries that could not be supported within the CXP.
  • Finalized, staged and launched changes to the IRCC site for the CXP R1.
  • Monitored and provided daily updates on user activity within visitor visa/super visa and CXP site pages
  • Liaised with the Passport team and DPM3 to prepare for R2

  • Most clients continue to apply through the IRCC secure account, disregarding the other two options available to them.

  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Description

  • Redesign project to simplify the navigation of the Work topic and help users get the right instructions to apply for their specific situation, given the complexity of the work permit landscape

What we did

  • Analyzed research from various data sources, including Reddit, Facebook, Page feedback tool, and Task success survey
  • Prototyped 4 templates for special work permit programs and provided a guidance document
  • Held a workshop to identify quick and big wins
  • Planned tree tests to evaluate existing and proposed information architecture (IA)

Next steps

  • Conduct tree tests and analyze results
  • Test two versions of band pattern in first-click tests
  • Implement new Work topic page based on testing results
  • Finish revamp of main work permit H2A
  • Integrate “Find out if you need a work permit” tool into main H2A to provide users with dynamic content relevant to their situation

What we learned

  • The complexity of work permits, with the various special programs, may not be suitable to a simple subway pattern and may need some out-of-the-box thinking
  • A phased approach is best to get some quick wins live while working towards the bigger win at the same time

My application

  • 1 Research
  • 2 Design
  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Description

  • Project to investigate client pain points and other issues related to general application content and how to improve the My application topic page.

What we did

  • Mapped out all general application content, including:
    • content linked off the My application topic page
    • content available in the Help Centre
  • Reviewed and analyzed data from
    • Adobe Analytics
    • the Page Feedback Tool
    • the GC Task Success Survey
    • Social Care
    • an environmental scan (Reddit, Twitter, Facebook, Canadavisa forum)
  • Identified recurring issues identified across these data sources and grouped them into categories:
    • issues we can’t fix
    • issues we’re already working to solve
    • new issues we can work on
  • Developed a presentation, outlining the research results and the issues/possible solutions we identified.

Next steps

  • Present the results to the rest of the WURD team and web management
  • Begin redesign of the My application topic page
  • Begin analysis of information in the Help Centre and develop a plan to merge that content into the main site

What we learned

  • The vast majority of general information about IRCC applications is not found within the main site, or under the My application topic.
  • Clients are looking for information about:
    • how to prepare documents and applications
    • how to update and correct applications they already submitted
    • how to find programs and the overall process
  • Accounts, application status, contacting IRCC and processing times continue to be issues that web can’t fully resolve.

Settlement

  • 1 Research
  • 2 Design
  • 3 Testing
  • 4 Launch
  • 5 Monitoring

Description

  • Look into the current state of the settlement content (directed at newcomers/ new immigrants), to help the advisors plan an IA revamp for this section.

What we did

  • Visually mapped out the current IA in Figjam, and shared it with the advisors, making the IA issues very clear for them to see
  • Performed research:
    • Used the existing research provided by the advisors to identify more problem areas in the Settlement content and IA
    • Conducted additional research that complimented and reinforced a lot of the initial research provided by the advisors
  • Shared the full research Figjam with advisors and walked them through the findings. We were able to agree on key issues, and they were able to pick what recommendations were most important/ relevant to them
  • Created a condensed report that highlights the main findings and the recommendations agreed upon by the advisors.

Next steps

  • Check in with advisors to assess the progress of the recommendation implementation
  • Revisit the data from the pages to observe performance improvements if any

What we learned

  • Information related to PR cards was found to be the bulk of the problem, and would require a project of its own to tackle.
  • Presenting the ‘raw’ research in an organized Figjam format can be beneficial for more focused asks like this. It really helped the advisors see the problems clearly and allowed them to prioritize the recommendations with us.
