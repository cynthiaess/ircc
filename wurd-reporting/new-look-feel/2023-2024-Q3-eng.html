Web UX Research and Design
Report - Q3 2023-2024

Creating client-centered products and supporting Web

The Web UX Research and Design (WURD) team has delivered improvements to the client experience in significant ways this quarter:

  • Launching the Explore immigration programs tool, which has been visited by over 30,000 clients already
  • Using evidence to support Tier 1 web content and advisors in their work, with more agility
  • Ensuring the smoothest transition to the Client eXperience Platform (CXP) possible

WURD has become more integrated with the broader web team, functioning in parallel and providing timely support. We also templated many of our research products, allowing faster and more repeatable work.

Projects

  • Explore immigration programs tool
  • Sign in
  • Visit Canada
  • Home Child Care Provider and Home Support Worker pilots
  • APR Portal research
  • Preparing for the Client Experience Platform
  • Web performance dashboard

Key highlights

Designing products of all sizes
WURD has successfully launched a number of products to improve client experience. These products range from large tools that required months of development and testing (like the Explore tool), to rapid page updates to communicate more clearly to clients (like our work on the Caregiver pages)

Sign in
WURD continues to be at the forefront of trying to improve the crucial client tasks of finding the right account and signing in. Sign in is a top IRCC task.

Preparing for the Client Experience Platform

Impact on the user journey

  1. Seek (impacted)
  2. Apply (impacted)
  3. Wait (impacted)
  4. Move forward (impacted)
  • Project to:
    • support the development of the new client platform for IRCC
    • coordinate the integration of this new platform into the existing IRCC website

What we did

  • Provided support and advice on the design and content for the overall platform, as well as for the specific application being released. Reviewed and provided advice on communications to be sent out from the system.
  • Liaised with project contacts to determine how the CXP will integrate with existing application-related tasks (including updating information, contacting the department, checking status, etc.)
  • Assessed risks of CXP roll-out plan and decommissioning of existing legacy platforms and provided guidance and advice to strategy team.

Next steps

  • Stage and launch site updates for CXP release 1
  • Provide support and advice on platform and content development for release 2

What we learned

  • While the long-term result of having only one account for the department is needed, in the short- and medium-term, the launch of a new account will increase confusion and risks for clients. It will continue to be difficult to impossible to tell clients which account they used to apply, and where they need to go to check their application status.
  • Lifting and shifting content from existing accounts does not guarantee the design and content are in their best state. Time should be built in to allow for thorough review and vetting with the appropriate program and policy stakeholders.
