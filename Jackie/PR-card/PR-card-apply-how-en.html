How to apply

What are you trying to do?

  • Get a PR card for the first time

    Are you within 180 days of receiving PR status?

    • Yes
    • No
  • Renew an existing PR card
  • Update information on my card

    What information do you need to update?

    • Correct a mistake
    • Correct date of birth
    • Change name
  • Replace a lost, stolen, or damaged PR card

    You have 2 options

    • Apply for a new card
    • Submit a solemn declaration

Get a PR card for the first time

Most people don’t need to apply for their first PR card. We automatically send you your first PR card if you give us your Canadian mailing address and photo within 180 days of becoming a permanent resident.

Give us your address within the first 180 days of becoming a Permanent Resident

The way to give us your information depends on how you confirm your permanent resident status.

If you confirmed your status at a port of entry

You need to give us your address (or update your address, if applicable) within 180 days of becoming a permanent resident.

Give or update your address online

If you are in Canada and confirm your status virtually

You need to give your Canadian address and upload a digital photo when you confirm your permanent residence in the PR portal.

Confirm your permanent residence and give us your address

Get a PR card for the first time

Most people don’t need to apply for their first PR card. We automatically send you your first PR card if you give us your Canadian mailing address and photo within 180 days of becoming a permanent resident.

If you were not able to get us your address within 180 days, you need to apply for a PR card.

1. Start your application in the Permanent Residence Portal

In the portal, begin your application by filling out the application for a permanent resident card (IMM 5444).

Need help? Our instruction guide (IMM 5445) has detailed information on how to prepare, complete and submit your application.

Sign in to the Permanent Residence portal

Special circumstances

There are different processes available under specific circumstances.

Apply for urgent processing

Urgent processing is available in some circumstances. However, the minimum processing time is 3 weeks. Even if you qualify for urgent processing, we can’t guarantee you’ll get your card on time.

Apply for urgent processing

Apply on paper, if you can’t apply online

If you can’t apply online and need accommodations, including for accessibility, you can apply on paper.

How to submit a paper application
  1. Complete the forms
    • IMM 5444 form
    • Document checklist IMM 5644
    • Any additional forms that apply to you
  2. Pay the CAN$50 fee online and include your receipt with your application.

We cannot refund the fee once the application is in process.

Send your application with all the required documents to the Case Processing Centre in Sydney.

By courier:
Case Processing Centre – PR Card
49 Dorchester Street
Sydney, NS B1P 5Z2
Canada

By mail (non-courier):
Case Processing Centre — PR Card
P.O. Box 10020
Sydney, NS B1P 7C1
Canada

2. Complete additional forms and upload them to the portal

Complete the Document checklist [IMM 5644] and upload it to the portal.

Depending on your circumstances, you may need to download, complete, and upload other forms to support your application.

Forms that may be required, depending on your situation:

You’re using a representative
  • Use of a representative (IMM 5476)
  • Authority to release personal information to a designated individual (IMM 5475)
You’re updating the gender identifier on your card
  • Request form for a change of sex or gender identifier (IRM 0002)
You’re reclaiming an Indigenous name
  • Confirmation of eligibility for a reclaimed name change gratis replacement document under Call to Action 17 (IRM 0004)
  • Statutory declaration to reclaim an Indigenous name on Canadian citizenship certificates or permanent resident cards (IRM 0005)
  • An amended birth certificate or legal change of name document

3. Pay your fees, then upload the receipt to the portal

Fees are not paid through the Permanent Residence Portal.

  1. Pay your fee online with IRCC.
  2. Upload your receipt to the Fee Payment section in the Permanent Residence Portal to show that you have paid.

Refunds are not possible while the application is in progress.

4. Review your application, then submit

Before you submit your application, make sure you:

  • answer all questions
  • electronically sign your application and all forms (type your full name exactly as shown on your passport)
  • include your processing fee receipt
  • upload all the supporting documents

5. Get updates about your application

Track the status of your application.

Renew an existing card

A PR card is usually valid for 5 years. In some cases, it can be valid for one year.

Renew your PR card if:

  • it will expire in less than 9 months, or
  • has expired
