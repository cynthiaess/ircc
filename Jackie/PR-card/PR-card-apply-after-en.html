Getting your PR card after you apply

What happens after we process your application and what to do once you get your PR card.

On this page:

  • How you’ll get your card
  • After you get your PR card
  • When to use your card
  • Start keeping track of your trips outside Canada

How you’ll get your card

If we approve your application, we’ll usually mail you your PR card. We only send PR cards to Canadian addresses.

If you live in a rural location where mail isn’t delivered directly to houses, we can only mail PR cards to post office (PO) boxes. We can’t mail it to a third party.

If you move or get a new phone number after submitting your application, contact us.

We may ask you to pick up your PR card at one of our offices. If we do, bring:

  • your passport or other travel document
  • the original versions of any documents you submitted copies of when you applied

If you don’t pick up your card within 180 days, we’ll destroy it and you’ll have to:

  • apply for a new one
  • pay the fee

After you get your PR card

When you get your card:

  • Check the card for mistakes
    • If there is a mistake on your card, you may be able to get your PR card replaced.
  • Destroy your old card, if you had one.

Didn’t get your card?

It can take up to 6 weeks beyond the normal processing time for you to get your permanent resident card.

If you didn’t get your card in:

  • 6 weeks but less than 180 days
    • Complete a solemn declaration.
  • 180 days or more
    • Apply for another PR card.
    • Be sure to fill in section G (solemn declaration) and include the fee payment.

When to use your PR card

You should always have a valid PR card before leaving Canada. You need it to re-enter Canada.

Have your PR card ready at:

  • land borders when travelling by car, truck, motorcycle, or RV
  • airports, train stations, and ports

If you leave Canada without a valid PR card, you will have to apply for a permanent resident travel document to return to Canada.

Start keeping track of your trips outside Canada

When renewing your PR card, or if you apply for Canadian citizenship, you need to tell us when you left Canada during your PR status, and for how long, for up to 5 years.

We have a travel journal you can download for free to help you keep track of your trips.
