Replacing a passport or other travel document

Answer a few additional questions to find out how to replace your passport or travel document.

You need to apply for a new certificate

To replace a lost, stolen, or damaged card or certificate, you need to apply for a new citizenship certificate.

We stopped giving cards in 2012, so if your card was lost, stolen, or damaged, you should still apply for a certificate.

If your card or certificate was stolen, you should also report the theft to your local police department.

You have to apply for a new PR card

Apply for a new PR card.

You can apply for a new RPCD if you are still in Canada.

Apply for a new Refugee Claimant Protection Document.

You can apply for Permanent residence

If your refugee claim or PRRA was approved, then you don’t need to replace your refugee protection claimant document. Instead, you can apply for permanent residence.

Contact Library and Archives Canada

You need to contact Library and Archives Canada for further instructions on how to obtain verification of your document.

Contact The Rooms

When you landed in Newfoundland before 1949, you must contact The Rooms for further instructions on how to obtain verification of your document.

Apply for a Replacement of an Immigration Document (IMM 5545)

Complete the Application for a Verification of Status (VOS) or Replacement of an Immigration Document (IMM 5545) and submit the application to the Operations Support Centre.

Apply for a Verification of Status

We will not replace this document. Instead, you can apply for a Verification of Status (VOS). A VOS document will contain information that appeared on your original imigration document. This document can be used when you must provide proof of historical immigration information, such as the date and place of entry to Canada.

Complete the Application for a Verification of Status (VOS) or Replacement of an Immigration Document (IMM 5545) and submit the application to the Operations Support Centre.

Note that the VOS cannot be used for travel and is not an identity document.
