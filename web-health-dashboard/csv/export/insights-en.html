Traffic to IRCC sites is slightly lower compared to last month.

Ensuring IRCC content is mobile-first remains critical as more than half of visits are on mobile devices.

2 of the top 5 mobile pages were about asylum claimants.

Note: Pages with less than 1K visits in a month are excluded.

Search engines (mainly Google) continue to drive most traffic to IRCC content, highlighting the importance of SEO (search engine optimization).

Of the traffic coming from social networks, over half of traffic is referred from Facebook.

  • Signing in remains a top task, as 1 in 5 visits includes visiting the secure sign-in landing page.
  • Users want to manage their applications. Of the topics on the Immigration theme page, over half of the clicks are on the My application link.
  • Visitor visa is clearly the most popular Visit program receiving nearly 3/4 of the doormat clicks.
  • Express Entry remains the most popular immigration program, receiving 1/3 of the doormat clicks on the Immigrate topic page.
  • Nearly half of doormat clicks on the Get a work permit page are for regular work permits for temporary workers.

Overall scores for task success, ease, and satisfaction have decreased slightly, but remain stable.

Check processing times continues to be the best performing task with a task success score of 86%.

Contact us

The Passport Help Centre and the Client Support Centre services pages continue to drive the most clicks on the Contact Us link in the footer.

Help Centre

The Contact IRCC pages, as well as the Minister's message, continue to get the most clicks on the Help Centre link.

Notes:

  • Help Centre link in footer is contextualized.
  • Pages with less than 1K visits in a month are excluded.
  • Main "Contact IRCC" and Help Centre content is excluded.

The news release about caregiver pilot programs becoming permanent continues to be the most visited news release for the 3rd month in a row, representing about 1/3 of traffic to all news releases.

The web notice about the work permit public policy for visitors continues to be the most visited web notice for the 3rd month in a row, representing about 3/4 of traffic to all web notices.

The most popular special measures content continues to be the immigration measures for Ukraine.

Traffic to special measures content has increased since last month, but remains under 1% of all IRCC traffic as usual.

The most popular campaign pages continue to be about eTAs for Mexican citizens and Francophone immigration.

The UTM campaign driving the most traffic to IRCC content is now the eTA/visitor visa campaign for Mexican citizens.
