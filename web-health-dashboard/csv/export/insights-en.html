Traffic to IRCC sites is slightly lower compared to last month.

Search engines (mainly Google) continue to drive most traffic to IRCC content, highlighting the importance of SEO (search engine optimization).

Of the traffic coming from social networks, over half of traffic is referred from Facebook.

  • Signing in remains a top task, as 1 in 5 visits includes visiting the secure sign-in landing page.
  • Users want to manage their applications. Of the topics on the Immigration theme page, over half of the clicks are on the My application link.
  • Visitor visa is clearly the most popular Visit program receiving nearly 3/4 of the doormat clicks.
  • Express Entry remains the most popular immigration program, receiving 1/3 of the doormat clicks on the Immigrate topic page.
  • Nearly half of doormat clicks on the Get a work permit page are for regular work permits for temporary workers.

Overall scores for task success, ease, and satisfaction have increased slightly but remain stable.

These tasks had the biggest increase in task success compared to August: Check your application status, Immigrate through Express Entry, Apply for an eTA.

Contact IRCC continues to be a low performing task with only about half of users able to complete their task.

Contact us

The Passport Help Centre and the Client Support Centre services pages continue to drive the most clicks on the Contact Us link in the footer.

Help Centre

The Contact IRCC pages, with the addition of the Contact IEC page, drove the most clicks on the Help Centre link in the footer.

Over half the traffic to news releases is going to the news release about decreasing the number of temporary residents.

The 2 most popular web notices are about the public policy on work permits for visitors (one is recent from August 2024, but the other notice dates back to February 2023).

The most popular special measures content continues to be the immigration measures for Ukraine.

Traffic to special measures content decreased since August, but remains under 1% of all IRCC traffic as usual.

The most popular campaign is about eTAs and visitor visas for Mexican citizens.

More than half of visits are on mobile devices.

4 of the top 5 mobile pages were about asylum claimants.

Note: Pages with less than 1K visits in a month are excluded.
