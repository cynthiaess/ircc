Traffic in December decreased by 14%, likely due to the holidays (a dip in traffic in winter is normal behaviour for GC sites).

Search engines (mainly Google) continue to drive most traffic to IRCC content, highlighting the importance of SEO (search engine optimization).

Of the traffic coming from social networks, over half of traffic is referred from Facebook.

  • Signing in remains a top task, as 1 in 5 visits includes visiting the secure sign-in landing page.
  • Users want to manage their applications. Of the topics on the Immigration theme page, nearly half of the clicks are on My application.
  • Visitor visa is clearly the most popular Visit program receiving over 65% of the doormat clicks.
  • Express Entry remains the most popular immigration program, receiving 1/3 of the doormat clicks on the Immigrate topic page.
  • Over half of doormat clicks on the Get a work permit page are for regular work permits for temporary workers.

Task success, ease and satisfaction scores have slightly increased but remain stable.

Check processing time is the best-performing task again. Contact IRCC continues to be the lowest-performing task.

Help Centre

The Contact IRCC page has the highest percentage of visits that click on the Help Centre link in the footer.

The top news release was about reducing fraud in the Express Entry program.

The top web notice was about the Immigration Levels Plan.

Traffic to special measures content decreased by over 10%.

The most popular special measures content continues to be the immigration measures for Ukraine.

The top campaign continues to be about eTAs and visitor visas for Mexican citizens.

More than half of visits are on mobile devices.

The page with the highest percentage of mobile visits is about travelling to Canada from India.
