IRCC Secure Account: Sign in

There are different accounts to use to apply, depending on the application you submit.

Before you use this account

Not all applications are available in the IRCC secure account. Before you create an account or sign in, check if you should be using this account:

When to use this secure account

You can use this account to apply online, check your application status and upload requested documents for some applications.

  • review, check the status & read messages for applications you've submitted
  • apply for some types of visitor visas - get visitor visa instructions for your situation
  • start or continue an application for work and study permits, Express Entry, and International Experience Canada
    • you will need your personal reference code if you already have one
    • you can also submit and pay for your application, get messages related to your application, check the status of your application and update your information
    • start an application to extend your stay
  • if you're already a citizen: apply for a proof of citizenship certificate or search for your citizenship record
  • if you are a protected person or refugee claimant: apply for the Interim Federal Health Program
  • if you are an approved study permit holder: transfer schools
    • you will need your application number

Find another IRCC account or portal

Get a personal reference code

You need this code if you use this account to apply

  • for Express Entry (to immigrate as a skilled worker)
  • to visit, study or work in Canada, including
    • extending your visa or permit
    • restoring your status
    • changing the conditions of your permit
    • International Experience Canada (IEC)

Use the Come to Canada tool to get the code.

Delays with the status of your medical exam results
If we asked you for a medical exam after you applied

You may not see the status of your results updated right away.

  • Your results usually show up in your account within 30 days of the date you booked your exam.
  • It may take longer (for example, if we need more information from you).
If you got your medical exam before you applied (upfront medical exam)

You may not see the status of your results updated in your account until we finish processing your immigration application. We need time to

  • process your application
  • link the results to your application

Check the application processing time.

If we ask or if a panel physician asks you for additional medical information, tests or treatment

To avoid more delays, you must

  • go back to see the physician
  • complete the requirement before we can assess your medical exam

Don’t complete a new medical exam unless we instruct you to do so.

Your panel physician can explain how long it will take to complete additional testing or treatment.

Errors and issues when you sign in

You can use the same GCKey username and password on different computers, phones and tablets.

If you're getting error messages:

  • use an updated modern web browser like Safari, Chrome, Firefox or Microsoft Edge, rather than Internet Explorer
  • don't use a favourite link or bookmark to pages inside the account - you must sign in to see them
  • use your browser's default settings because GCKey needs JavaScript and cookies enabled

If you see one of these messages when you try to sign in, update your browser:

  • Page Can’t Be Displayed
  • Cannot Establish a Secure Connection
  • Page Not Available

You can usually find the option to update your browser in your browser menu's settings or preferences. If you are using Internet Explorer, update your browser to Microsoft Edge.

Other technical issues
  • Too many attempts: If you make too many unsuccessful attempts to sign in during the same session, you may see a failure message. Close your browser window or tab and start again.
  • Experiencing technical difficulties: The site could be down for a number of reasons, including a brief period of system maintenance. We apologize for the inconvenience. Try again later.
  • Session expired: For security, your session will be closed if it's inactive for a certain number of minutes. Sign in again.
Your GCKey password or username

Read the instructions for your situation.

Forgot your username and password
  1. Follow the steps to recover your username first.
  2. Once you have your username, follow the steps to recover your password.
Forgot your username
If you have a recovery email address
  1. Select GCKey username and password.
  2. Select Forgot your username? on the Welcome to GCKey page.
  3. Enter your recovery email address on the Recover Username Step 1 of 2 page.
    • You'll be brought to a page that explains where we’ll email your username and what to do if you don't receive the email.
  4. Select Continue.
  5. Check your email. You should get an email from the Government of Canada with your GCKey username.
  6. Sign in with your username and password on the Welcome to GCKey page.
If you don’t have a recovery email address
  1. Select GCKey username and password.
  2. Select the Sign up button on the Welcome to GCKey page to create a new GCKey username and password.
  3. Create a new account in the IRCC secure account.

During this process, you may be able to recover your account or link your existing applications to your new account.

Forgot your password
  1. Select GCKey username and password.
  2. Select Forgot your password? on the Welcome to GCKey page.
  3. Enter your GCKey username.
  4. Select Continue.
    • If you used an email address when signing up for your GCKey username, then reset your password with your email address.
    • If you didn't use an email address to sign up, then reset your password using recovery questions.
Password reset using an email address
  1. Enter your email address, then check your email account. You should get an email from the Government of Canada with a confirmation code.
  2. Enter the confirmation code on the Forgot Your Password Step 2 of 4 page.
    • This page should load automatically after you enter your username and select Continue on the previous page.
  3. Select Continue.
  4. Enter the answer to the recovery question you’re prompted with in the My Recovery Answer field.
  5. Select Continue.
  6. Enter a new password in the New Password field.
  7. Re-enter that same password in the Confirm New Password field.
  8. Select Continue.
  9. Select Continue again on the Forget Your Password Complete page.
Password reset using recovery questions
  1. Select the Recovery Q&A button in the Recovery Questions and Answers box on the Forget Your Password Step 2 of 4 page.
  2. Enter your answers in the My Recovery Answer, My Memorable Person Answer and My Memorable Date fields.
    • You should find the questions and hints above each field.
  3. Enter a new password in the Create Your New Password field.
  4. Enter the same password in the Confirm Your New Password field.
  5. Select Continue.
  6. Select Continue again on the Forget Your Password Complete page.
    • If you correctly answered all the recovery questions, you'll automatically be brought to this page.
You lost or no longer use the email you registered with
  1. Sign up for GCKey again.
  2. Create a new account in the IRCC secure account.

You may not be able to find your application in your new account. You need to link your application to your new account.

GCKey multi-factor authentication

Multi-factor authentication protects your account. You need to choose a second authentication method (other than your username and password) to use each time you sign in.

Set up your multi-factor authentication method

Change or recover your multi-factor authentication method

You need access to your existing email or authenticator app, or to your saved recovery codes.

If you want to change your multi-factor authentication device or method
  1. Sign in to your account with GCKey.
  2. Select Manage your Two-Factor Authentication on the Welcome page.
  3. Select Change your two-factor authenticationauthentication success page.
  4. Follow the prompts to set up multi-factor authentication again.
  5. Save your new recovery codes somewhere safe. You old codes will no longer work.
If you need to recover your multi-factor authentication method
  1. Sign in to your account with GCKey.
  2. Select Recover account on the multi-factor authentication page.
  3. Enter one of the recovery codes you saved when you first set up multi-factor authentication.
  4. Select Change your two-factor authenticationauthentication success page.
  5. Follow the prompts to set up multi-factor authentication again.
  6. Save your new recovery codes somewhere safe. You old codes will no longer work.

If you lose your recovery codes, you must:

  1. Sign up for a new GCKey username and password.
  2. Create a new account in the IRCC secure account.

Get more help with multi-factor authentication

GCKey revoked

You can’t recover your GCKey if it was revoked. It can be revoked if:

  • your username and password are compromised, or
  • you haven’t used GCKey in the last 2 years
    • Don’t use the same username and password you use for GCKey for any other online accounts.

You can choose a different sign-in method or sign up for GCKey again.

To sign up for GCKey again:

  1. Select GCKey username and password.
  2. Select the Sign up button on the Welcome to GCKey page to create a new GCKey username and password.
  3. Create a new account in the IRCC secure account.

During this process, you may be able to recover your account or link your existing applications to your new account.

Change your Sign-in Partner

If you use Sign-in Partner and you moved to a new bank or credit union, you may be able to switch your Sign-in Partner:

  1. Select Canadian Interac® Sign-in Partner.
  2. Select Switch My Sign-in Partner from the menu on the Select a Sign-in Partner page.
  3. Follow the steps to change your Sign-in Partner.
