1. Get a medical exam, if you need one

You generally don’t need a medical exam unless you plan to work in healthcare or with children, or if you've lived in certain countries or territories.

Find out if you need a medical exam

2. Sign in to your account

Sign in to your IRCC secure account to get your personalized document checklist and apply online.

Sign in to your account

Before you apply online, you’ll need:

  • a scanner or camera to create electronic copies of your documents
  • a valid credit or debit card

Credit cards and prepaid cards

We accept credit cards and prepaid cards from:

  • Visa®
  • MasterCard®
  • American Express®
  • JCB®
  • UnionPay® (for online applications only; debit cards not accepted)

If you use a prepaid credit card, keep it for at least 18 months after you pay to make refunds easier. Find more on prepaid cards.

We also accept all Visa Debit cards.The card you use doesn't have to be in your name. The cardholder’s name will appear on the receipt but it does not need to match your application.

Debit cards

It must:

  • be from a Canadian bank using INTERAC® Online, and
  • be registered for online banking through your bank’s website.

We also accept all Debit MasterCard® and Visa® Debit cards. If your card has the Visa Debit logo on it, alone or with the INTERAC® Online logo, select Visa Debit as your payment method.

The card you use does not have to be in your name. The cardholder’s name will appear on the receipt but it does not need to match your application.

If you can't apply online and need accommodations

You can only apply on paper if you:

  • have a disability that prevents you from applying online,
  • are applying under the Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program, or
  • hold an identity or travel document for non-national residents, refugees or stateless persons.

What to do if you have problems applying online

3. Check if you need special instructions

You may need special instructions depending on the eligibility criteria that you qualify under.

Continue your work permit application with the special instructions for your situation:

  • Family members of foreign workers
  • Spouses of international students
  • Post-graduation work permit (PGWP)
  • Permanent residence applicants (and their family members)
  • French-speaking workers outside Quebec
  • Vulnerable foreign workers
  • Hong Kong recent graduates

4. Complete forms and upload documents

Required forms

You must fill out and submit all of these forms:

  • Application to Change Conditions, Extend Your Stay or Remain in Canada as a Worker (IMM 5710) (opens in a new tab)
    • If you need help filling out the application form, read the instruction guide.
  • Document Checklist (IMM 5556) (opens in a new tab)

Additional forms

You may need to also submit these forms if they apply to you:

  • Statutory Declaration of Common-law Union (IMM 5409) (PDF, 636.51KB), if applicable
  • Use of a Representative (IMM 5476) (opens in a new tab) , if applicable
  • Authority to release personal information to a designated individual [IMM 5475] (PDF, 593.57KB), if applicable

5. Pay the fees

We’ll ask you to pay your fees at the end of your application.

In most cases, your fees will include processing fees for you and anyone you include on your application.

You can expect to pay the following fees:

  • work permit fee ($155) (for empoyer-specific and open work permits)
  • open work permit holder fee ($100) (for open work permits only)
  • biometrics fee, if you need to give them

6. Give biometrics, if applicable

You may need to give your biometrics for this permit. If you need to give biometrics, pay the biometrics fee when you apply. The fee covers the cost of collecting fingerprints and a digital photo.

Find out if you need to give your biometrics.

Extend the validity of your biometrics

Your biometrics are valid for 10 years.

If your biometrics will expire before your work permit expires, you can give your biometrics again when you apply even if they’re still valid.

How to extend the validity of your biometrics:

  1. Pay the biometrics fee when you apply for your work permit.
  2. Wait for your biometric instruction letter (BIL). You must use the BIL that was issued for this application.
  3. Book an appointment and visit an official biometric collection location to give your biometrics again.
