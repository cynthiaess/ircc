Who can apply

Answer a few questions to check if you're eligible to apply for a work permit.

Find out what type of work permit you need and if there are special instructions to apply for your situation.

Where are you applying from?

  • Outside Canada

    Do you have a job offer?

    • Yes
    • No

      Do one of the following situations apply to you?

      • You're a family member of a foreign worker.
      • You're a Hong Kong recent graduate.
      • You or your family member is an H-1B visa holder.
      • You're a family member of an applicant under the temporary resident to permanent resident (TRPR) pathway.
      • You're a family member of an Agri-Food Pilot applicant.
      • You're a family member of an Atlantic Immigration Program applicant.
      • You're a family member of a Quebec investor.
      • None of the above situations apply to me.

        Are you between the ages of 18 and 35?

        • Yes
        • No
  • Inside Canada

    Do one of the following situations apply to you?

    • You have a valid work permit and you need an extension.
    • You have a valid study permit.
    • Your family member has a valid study or work permit.
    • You're a recent graduate from a designated learning institution (DLI).
    • You or your family member is a Ukrainian national.
    • You or your family member has a temporary resident permit that is valid for six months or more.
    • You or your family member is an asylum claimant.
    • You've been recognized as a convention refugee or protected person.
    • You’re a trader, investor, intra-company transferee or professional under the Canada – United States – Mexico Agreement (CUSMA).
    • None of the above situations apply to me.

You're eligible for a work permit

Your work permit will be employer-specific, meaning you can only work for the employer who made you the job offer.

You're eligible for a work permit

As a recent graduate from Hong Kong, you're eligible for an open work permit under a temporary public policy for Hong Kong residents.

An open work permit lets you work for any employer without a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA).

You're eligible for a work permit

As a temporary resident permit holder, you and your family members are eligible for a work permit.

Apply for a work permit

You're eligible for a work permit

As an asylum claimant, you and your family members are eligible for an open work permit.

An open work permit lets you work for any employer without a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA).

If you haven’t already submitted your asylum claim, you can request a work permit in your online claim application. You don't need to apply for a work permit separately.

If you already submitted your asylum claim, but didn’t apply for a permit at the same time, you can apply for a work permit separately.
