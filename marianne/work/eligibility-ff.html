Employer-specific work permit

Based on your answers, you need an employer-specific work permit to work in Canada.

You need a job offer to get an employer-specific work permit. You'll only be able to work for the employer who made you the job offer.

Open work permit

Based on your answers, you need an open work permit to work in Canada.

An open work permit lets you work for any employer in Canada.

Bridging open work permit

Based on your answers, you need a bridging open work permit (BOWP) to work in Canada.

A BOWP lets you keep working while you wait for the results of your permanent residence application.

You don't need a work permit

Based on your answers, you don't need a work permit to work in Canada.

You may need a visitor visa or eTA to enter Canada.

You don't need a work permit

You can work on campus as an international student if you meet all the eligibility criteria.

Check if you're eligible to work on campus

You don't need a work permit

You can work off-campus as an international student if you meet all the eligibility criteria.

Check if you're eligible to work off-campus

Co-op work permit

Based on your answers, you may be eligible for a co-op work permit, which is an open work permit that lists your school as your employer.

Eligibility

You must meet all of these conditions:

  • you have a valid study permit
  • you're required to work to complete your study program in Canada
  • you have a letter from your school that confirms all students in your program need to complete work placements to get their degree
  • your co-op placement or internship totals 50% or less of your study program

Who's not eligible

You aren’t eligible for a co-op work permit if you’re taking one of the following:

  • English or French as a second language (ESL/FSL),
  • general interest courses, or
  • courses to prepare for another study program.

If you’re not eligible for a co-op work permit, but you want to work in Canada, you can apply for a regular work permit.

Post-graduation work permit (PGWP)

Based on your answers, you may be eligible for a post-graduation work permit. This is a type of open work permit.

Check if you're eligible for a PGWP

International Experience Canada (IEC)

Based on your answers, you may be eligible for a work permit under one of International Experience Canada's categories:

  • Working Holiday (open work permit)
  • International Co-op (Internship) (employer-specific work permit)
  • Young Professionals (employer-specific work permit)
Check if you're eligible for IEC

You don't need a work permit

You don't need a work permit if you’re coming to Canada for business activities, but you won’t be part of the Canadian labour market.

Business visitors are people who stay in Canada for a few days or a few weeks to attend meetings, events or conferences. They can stay in Canada for up to 6 months.

Get instructions for business visitors

Eligibility

To be eligible for a BOWP, you must

  • live in Canada (and intend to live outside Quebec) at the time you apply for your BOWP
    • You can leave Canada while we process your application.
    • However, if you leave Canada after your work permit expires, you can’t work until your new application is approved.
  • either
    • have a valid work permit
    • have an expired work permit but maintained your status as a worker
    • be eligible to restore your status and get a work permit
  • be the principal applicant on your permanent residence application
  • be approved in principle for permanent residence

Eligibility

This work permit is only valid for 2 years.

To qualify for this work permit, you need:

  • a job offer from a designated employer
  • a referral letter from the Atlantic province where you’ll be working
  • to meet the requirements for:
    • language
    • education
    • work experience (unless you’re an international graduate)

Eligibility

You may be eligible for an open work permit if you filed a claim for refugee protection in Canada and you meet these conditions:

  • your claim has been found eligible and you’re waiting for the Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada to decide on your claim
  • you cannot pay for your basic needs without working
  • you have valid immigration medical exam results
    • we won’t issue your work permit until you pass your immigration medical exam

When to apply

Normally, you’ll apply for your open work permit when you make your claim for refugee protection.

If you didn’t apply for a work permit when you made your claim, you can apply later.

Eligibility

You need a work permit if you’re appealing a decision from the Immigration Refugee Board or you’re under a removal order and cannot be removed from Canada.

You must meet these requirements:

  • you cannot pay for your basic needs without working
  • you meet the general eligibility requirements for a work permit

Public policy

As a recent Hong Kong graduate, you may be eligible to apply for an open work permit under a temporary public policy for Hong Kong residents.

The public policy expires on February 7, 2025.

Validity of work permit

Your work permit will be valid for up to 3 years or until your passport expires, whichever comes first.

For example, if your passport is valid for 6 months, then you will be issued an open work permit for 6 months.

Eligibility

To be eligible, you must:

  • hold a valid passport issued by the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region or the United Kingdom to a British National Overseas
  • have graduated no more than 10 years before you apply for this open work permit

Acceptable credentials

You must have graduated from a designated learning institution (DLI) in Canada or an institution abroad with one of these credentials:

  • a degree (for example, associate, bachelor’s, master’s or doctoral)
  • a diploma from a program of at least 2 years
  • a graduate or post-graduate credential that:
    • is from a program of at least one year, and
    • required you to complete a post-secondary degree or diploma, as a prerequisite for acceptance into the program
      • You must have received the post-secondary degree or diploma no more than 5 years before you started the graduate/post-graduate program.

Pay the fees

You’re exempt from paying the open work permit holder fee (CAN$100). Don’t pay this fee.

You must pay the work permit processing fee (CAN$155).

Because you’re exempt from paying one of the fees, you need to pay your fees before you apply online.

  1. Go to fees for temporary residence applications.
  2. Select “Work permit (including extensions) – per person”.
  3. Enter the number of applications you’re paying for.
  4. Select "Submit". The system will calculate the total amount you need to pay.
  5. Keep a copy of the receipt. You’ll need to upload it under “Proof of Fee Exemption” when you apply online for your work permit.

Step 5. Give biometrics, if applicable

You may need to give your biometrics for this permit. If you need to give biometrics, pay the biometrics fee when you apply. The fee covers the cost of collecting fingerprints and a digital photo.

Find out if you need to give your biometrics

Extend the validity of your biometrics

Your biometrics are valid for 10 years.

If your biometrics will expire before your work permit expires, you can give your biometrics again when you apply even if they’re still valid.

How to extend the validity of your biometrics

  1. Pay the biometrics fee when you apply for your work permit.
  2. Wait for your biometric instruction letter (BIL). You must use the BIL that was issued for this application.
  3. Book an appointment and visit an official biometric collection location to give your biometrics again.
