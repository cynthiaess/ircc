Requirements by location

Outside Canada

You must:

  • prove to an officer that you will leave Canada when your work permit expires
  • show that you have enough money to take care of yourself and your family members during your stay in Canada and to return home
  • be in good health and have a medical exam, if needed
  • give the officer any other documents they ask for to prove you can enter the country

Inside Canada

General requirements

You must:

  • prove to an officer that you will leave Canada when your work permit expires
  • show that you have enough money to take care of yourself and your family members during your stay in Canada and to return home
  • be in good health and have a medical exam, if needed

Specific situations you can apply under

Most people can't apply for a work permit within Canada.

You can only apply within Canada if one of these situations applies to you:

  • You have a valid work permit that needs to be extended.
    • Extend your work permitOpens in a new tab
  • Your family member has a valid work permit.
    • Check eligibility for family members of foreign workersOpens in a new tab
  • You or your family member has a valid study permit.
    • Check eligibility for spouses of international studentsOpens in a new tab
  • You’re an international student who graduated from certain designated learning institutions.
    • Check eligibility for a post-graduation work permit (PGWP)Opens in a new tab
  • You have a temporary resident permit (TRP) that is valid for 6 months or more.
  • You applied for permanent residence in Canada.
  • You're a refugee, asylum claimant, or protected person.
  • You’ve been legally working in Canada without a work permit but you now need a work permit for a different job.
  • You’re a trader, investor, intra-company transferee or professional under the Canada – United States – Mexico Agreement (CUSMA).

Port of entry

You should apply for your work permit before you travel to Canada. However, you can apply for a work permit when you enter Canada if you’re eligible.

Requirements

To apply at a port of entry (POE), you must meet all of the following:

  • be from a visa-exempt country
  • be eligible for an electronic travel authorization (eTA) or to travel without a visitor visa
  • meet other requirements depending on the type of work permit you’re applying for

Who's not eligible

You cannot apply at a POE if you:

  • need a visitor visa
  • are a citizen of one of these eTA-eligible (visa-required) countries
  • are an International Experience Canada (IEC) applicant who is not a citizen or permanent resident of the United States
  • are applying under the Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program (SAWP)
  • are applying for a post-graduation work permit (PGWP)

Faster processing

You may also be eligible for two-week processing through the Global Skills Strategy.

Global Skills Strategy: Check if you're eligibleOpens in a new tab

Bringing family members with you

Your spouse, common-law partner and dependent children may be able to work, study or live with you while you work in Canada.

How to bring your family members to Canada with youOpens in a new tab

Who's not eligible for a work permit

You can't apply for a work permit if you:

  • have a record of criminal activity
    • We may ask you to give us a police clearance certificate.
  • are a danger to Canada’s security
  • plan to work for an employer listed with the status “ineligible” on the list of employers who failed to comply with the conditions
  • plan to work for an employer who, on a regular basis, offers striptease, erotic dance, escort services or erotic massages
