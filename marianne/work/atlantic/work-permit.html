Temporary work permit (optional)

On this page

  • Type of work permit
  • Work permits for spouses
  • Who can apply
  • Get your documents ready
  • How to apply
  • Next steps

You may be eligible to apply for a temporary work permit. The work permit lets you work while your permanent residence application is being processed.

If you're in Canada as a visitor

If you’re in Canada as a visitor only (you don’t currently have a study or work permit), find out how you can apply for a work permit from inside Canada.

Type of work permit

This work permit

  • is employer-specific, meaning it only allows you to work for the employer who offered you the job
  • is only for the Atlantic Immigration Program
  • is valid for 2 years
  • is exempt from a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA)

Getting a temporary work permit doesn’t automatically mean we’ll approve your permanent residence application.

Work permits for spouses or common-law partners

Your spouse or common-law partner can apply for an open work permit.

Who can apply

To qualify for a work permit, you must have:

  • a job offer from a designated employer that meets the requirements of the program
  • a referral letter from the Atlantic province where you’ll be working

If you apply for a temporary work permit, you need to show that you meet the requirements for:

  • language
  • education
  • work experience (unless you’re an international graduate)

Get your documents ready

If this is the first time you’re applying under the Atlantic Immigration Program, your application must include all of the following:

  • a job offer number (see Employer Portal for details)
    • When completing the Offer of Employment in the Employer Portal, the employer should indicate a duration of 2 years for the work permit.
  • your referral letter from the Atlantic province where you’ll be working
  • your signed IMM 0156 form (PDF, 254 KB)
  • language test results
  • educational credentials
  • proof of work experience

How to apply

The application forms and process you need to follow depend on where you’re applying from.

Outside Canada

1. Make sure you have what you need

To apply online, you’ll need

  • a scanner or camera to create electronic copies of your documents, and
  • a valid credit or debit card

2. Create an IRCC secure account or sign in

You need an IRCC secure account to apply online. You can use your account to:

  • pay your fees
  • submit your application
  • check your application status
Sign in to your account

3. Check your country-specific application requirements

Some countries have additional visa office requirements. Use the tool below to check if you have additional requirements.

4. Get your document checklist

After you sign in to your IRCC secure account,

  1. Select “Apply to come to Canada”.
  2. Select “Visitor visa, study and/or work permit” under "Determine your eligibility and apply online".

Answer the following questions exactly as shown.

Question Answer
What you would like to do in Canada? Work
How long are you planning to stay in Canada? Temporarily—more than 6 months
I have a job offer and my employer gave me an offer of employment number or LMIA number (or proof of that employer applied for an LMIA) Yes
What is the status of your job offer? Temporary—at least one year
What type of work permit do you want to apply for? A work permit with a Labour Market Impact Assessment exemption

5. Fill out the application form

Follow the instructions to complete your application and pay your fees.

If you need help filling out the application form, read the instruction guide.

6. Upload your documents

Scan and upload the documents from your personalized document checklist.

Client information

When you see a request for a document called “Client information”, upload the following documents as 1 scanned file

  • your referral letter
  • your signed IMM 0156 form
  • your educational credentials
  • your language test results (English or French)
  • your proof of work experience, if applicable
  • your additional visa office requirements, if applicable for your country

Find out how to upload multiple documents into 1 field.

7. Submit your application

After you complete your application, follow the online instructions to submit it.
