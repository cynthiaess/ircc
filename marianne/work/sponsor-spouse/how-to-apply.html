1. Make sure you have what you need

To apply online, you’ll need

  • a scanner or camera to create electronic copies of your documents
  • a valid credit or debit card

2. Sign in to your account

Sign in to your IRCC secure account to get your document checklist and apply online.

Sign in to your account
If you can't apply online and need accommodations

You can submit a paper application if either

  • you can’t apply online because of a disability
  • there’s a problem with the online application
    • In some cases, you may be able to apply online if you follow special instructions.

What to do if you have problems applying online

3. Get your document checklist

Once you sign in to your IRCC secure account, you must answer some questions to get a personalized document checklist.

Answer the following question exactly as shown.

Question Answer
Does one of the following apply to you?
  • I am the subject of a removal order that is unenforceable.
  • I applied for a study or work permit before entering Canada that was approved but not issued at a port of entry.
  • I am a student in primary or secondary school, or a visiting or exchange student, in Canada with visitor status.
  • I am applying for an open work permit under an active public policy or pilot program announced by IRCC.
 Yes

4. Fill out the application form

If you need help filling out the application form, read the instruction guide.

Fill out the work permit application form exactly as shown.

Question Answer
Box 1: What type of work permit are you applying for? Open Work Permit
Box 4: Job title SCLPC – FC OWP
Box 4: Brief description of duties SCLPC – FC applicant in Canada public policy

5. Upload your documents

The document checklist will include most of the documents you need. Below are the extra documents you must upload for this open work permit.

Find out how to upload multiple documents into one field.

Client information

In the Client information part of the document checklist:

  • Upload the Acknowledgement of Receipt (AOR) letter that shows that the permanent residence application is complete.
  • Upload proof that you (and your children) have valid temporary resident status (for example, a valid work permit, visitor record or study permit).
    • You only need to upload this proof if you’ve received your AOR.
If you're eligible to apply without an AOR letter

If you didn’t receive your AOR letter and you meet the requirements, upload both of the following instead:

  • a copy of your work permit, study permit or visitor record, and
  • proof showing you’ve submitted your permanent residence application
    • (for example, the email we sent you confirming that you’ve successfully submitted your permanent residence application)

Family information

In the Family Information form (IMM 5707) part of the document checklist, upload:

  • the Family Information form,
  • your marriage licence or certificate or the Statutory Declaration of Common-Law Union (IMM 5409), and
  • the birth certificates of your dependent children that show both parents’ names.

6. Pay your fees

In most cases, your fees will include processing fees for you and anyone you include on your application.

If you’re applying for an open work permit, make sure you pay all applicable fees:

  • work permit fee ($155)
  • open work permit holder fee ($100)
