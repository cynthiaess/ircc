Who can apply on paper

Most people have to apply online to extend their stay in Canada as a worker. You can only submit a paper application if:

  • you can’t apply online because of a disability, or
  • there’s a problem with the online application

Problem with the online application

If you’re trying to apply for a work permit online in one of the categories below, you may have problems getting the right result. If you’re not sure if this applies to you, ask your employer.

LMIA-exempt applicants

  • Permanent residence applicants in Canada who
    • used Express Entry to submit their permanent residence application, and
    • have received an acknowledgment of receipt letter (A75)
  • Open work permit pilot program for permanent residence applicants in the spouse or common-law partner in Canada class (A70)
  • Foreign physicians coming to work in Quebec (C10)
  • Refugee claimants (S61)
  • Failed refugee claimants waiting for removal who need to support themselves (S62)
  • Persons under an unenforceable removal order (S62)
  • Live-in caregivers who have submitted their permanent residence application (A71)
  • In-Canada applicants under the Home Child-Care Provider Pilot or the Home Support Worker Pilot (C90) and their family members (C91)
  • Post-graduation work permit extension (C43)
  • Destitute students (H81)
  • Holders of a temporary resident permit that’s valid for a minimum of 6 months (H82)
  • Start-up visa workers (A75)
  • International Experience Canada: Extend your participation or change conditions on your work permit (C21)

LMIA-required applicants

  • Seafood-processing workers
  • Agricultural workers who are not part of the Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program

Visitors to Canada

  • Visitors in Canada applying for an initial study permit, work permit or both who are accompanying a spouse holding a low-skilled work permit

International Experience Canada participants

If you’re extending your participation or changing your work permit under International Experience Canada (IEC), you must follow the IEC-specific instructions.

Go to instructions for IEC participants

Step 1: Fill out the forms

  • Application to Change Conditions or Extend Your Stay in Canada [IMM 5710] (opens in a new tab)
    • If you need help filling out the application form, read the instruction guide.
  • Statutory Declaration of Common-law Union [IMM 5409] (PDF, 0.80 MB), if applicable
  • Use of a Representative [IMM 5476] (opens in a new tab) , if applicable
  • Authority to release personal information to a designated individual [IMM 5475] (PDF, 1.52 MB), if applicable

How to download and complete the forms

  1. Use your computer (Mac or PC).
    • The form won’t open on mobile devices or tablets.
  2. Install Adobe Reader 10 (or higher).
  3. Download the PDF file to your computer. Save the file in a place you can remember.
    • Get help downloading files.
  4. Open the form using Adobe Reader and fill out all the form fields.
    • Filling out the form on a computer is easier than filling it out by hand.
  5. Select the Validate button at the top or bottom of the form.
    • This makes sure that you answered all the questions in the form and will help you avoid processing delays.
    • A new page with barcodes and a signature line will automatically be added at the end of your form.
      • Find out more about barcode forms.
  6. Print, sign and date the form.
    • Print on white, bond-quality, non-glossy paper.
    • Use a laser printer.
  7. Place the barcode page at the top of your application package.
    • If you have more than one package, place a separate barcode page on each one.

Step 2: Pay the fees

  1. Make sure you have what you need:
    • access to a printer, and
    • a credit card, Debit MasterCard® or Visa® Debit card
  2. Go to Online Payment and follow the online instructions.
  3. At the end, select the button to print the IRCC official receipt with barcode. Print two copies.
  4. Attach a copy of this receipt to your application. Keep the second copy of the receipt for your records.

Step 3: Mail the application

Get mailing instructions in the instruction guide.

Alternate formats

If you can’t apply online or use the forms above because of a disability, you can ask for other formats including large print and braille.

Contact the Client Support Centre (in Canada only) or submit a Web form for other formats.
