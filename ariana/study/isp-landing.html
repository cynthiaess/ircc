Updates for international students

Canada is proud to be a top destination of choice for international students, thanks to our:

  • quality educational institutions
  • welcoming and diverse society
  • opportunities to work or immigrate permanently after graduation

While students have contributed to life on campuses across the country, they’ve also experienced some challenges navigating Canada’s International Student Program. In collaboration with provinces and territories, we’ve introduced reforms to support students and protect program integrity.

Changes to protect students and improve program integrity

International students bring significant social, cultural and economic benefits to Canada. For those benefits to continue, we must address challenges to protect genuine students from fraud.

Enhanced letter of acceptance verification

Launched December 2023

To protect prospective students from fraud, post-secondary designated learning institutions (DLI) now confirm every study permit applicant’s letter of acceptance (LOA).

Learn more about LOA verification

Genuine Students Impacted by Fraud Taskforce

Launched June 2023

After receiving invalid LOAs with study permit applications, we formed a team with the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) to prevent genuine students from being removed from Canada. We’re working together to identify bad actors and stop them from taking advantage of genuine students.

Learn more about protecting yourself from fraud

Work permit eligibility for spouses and partners

Updated March 2024

Only the spouses and common-law partners of international students in the following programs are eligible for an open work permit:

  • graduate degrees (master’s and doctoral)
  • professional degree-granting

Learn more about the changes to spousal open work permits

Measures to support student success

In welcoming international students to Canada, we have a responsibility to make sure that students are supported and set up for success.

Updated financial requirement

Updated January 2024

The financial requirement for study permit applicants was raised on January 1, 2024, so that international students are prepared for the cost of living in Canada. This will be updated each year to help set students up for success.

Learn more about the updated financial requirements 

Longer PGWPs for master's degree graduates

Launched February 2024

Graduates of master’s degree programs are excellent candidates to succeed our labour market so we’re making it easier to qualify for permanent residence. On February 15, 2024, we introduced a longer, 3-year PGWP for students who are graduating from programs under 2 years.

Learn more about the changes to PGWP length for master's degree graduates 

Update to PGWP eligibility for public-private college partnership programs

Updated May 2024

Some provinces allow students to physically attend a private college but graduate with a public college’s diploma through a “curriculum licensing” agreement. Programs under these agreements enrolled a large amount of students in recent years, despite concerns about a lack of student support. Students who began this type of program after May 15, 2024 are not eligible for a PGWP.

Learn more about the changes to PGWP eligibility 

Distance learning

Available until September 2024

Until September 2024, international students can continue to count time spent studying online towards the length of a future post-graduation work permit (PGWP), as long as it’s less than 50% of the program of study.

Learn more about the updates to distance learning 

Changes to off-campus work hours

Coming Fall 2024

The temporary policy allowing students to work more than 20 hours per week off campus ended on April 30, 2024. Those on a scheduled academic break can continue working unlimited hours. This fall, we intend to increase the limit to 24 hours so students can focus on their studies, while having the option to work, if necessary.

Learn more about the changes to off-campus work hours 

Working with provinces and territories to stabilize growth

International students enrich Canada’s communities and are an important part of our social, cultural and economic fabric. In recent years, some institutions accepted more students than they could support and the integrity of the system was threatened. This has put pressure on housing, health care and other community services.

Student cap

Launched January 2024

To make sure we only accept as many students as Canada can support, study permit applications will be capped for two years. Each province and territory is allocated a number of applications under the cap.

Learn more about the cap

Provincial attestation letter

Launched January 2024

Most new post-secondary international students at the college or undergraduate level must include a PAL from their DLI in their study permit application.

Learn more about the PAL

Allocations

Announced March 2024

The 2024 allocations are based on expiring study permits – so the number of students coming to Canada should be the same as the number of expiring permits. Provinces and territories distribute their allocation to DLIs through provincial attestation letters (PAL).

Learn more about how the cap is allocated
